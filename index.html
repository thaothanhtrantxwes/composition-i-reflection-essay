My Reflection Essay

May 03, 2019

Now that I’m so close to the end of Dr. Myers’ Composition I class this spring semester 2019, it’s time to sit back and high-five my better self! Well, actually, how can I know if I’m better?!

So, I go over all my Composition I writing projects: from my personal essay, to teamwork profile series, to my summary and response essay, to teamwork informative article series;   I am now transfixed by whatever this feeling is—kind of strange, much irritating, and just glad. As so, I’m wording out this reflection essay as a way to slap me in the face.

What can I say? English is too much of a language for someone like me. All my life I struggle hard for the ‘I’ of what I see and the ‘a’ of every count in reality. Oops, that doesn’t make any sense. My point is…   I feel kind of strange probably because when I’m done reading what I wrote months ago, I see that I’m still the same in my heart, but at the same time I see the distance between then and now, my focus then and now.   I feel much irritating probably because what I wrote sounds so emo at times and some parts just sound dead-serious. I want fun now. And I want to sound like fun now.   I’m just glad probably because all to me now is not as I remembered it. All my writings were done last-minute, so I remembered each as how it could have been better. But now I’m just pretty chill. After all, it all sounds so-so. I no longer care as much.

Recap on my behaviors in class: Bad! Somehow, most of me was in view-only mode, my chill was elsewhere. My behaviors were about wrong, out of sync, might have been misunderstood. But whatever, I then chose to stay in my head for the tough times, and that is not what this essay is about.

Next on my study. The thing I love about this class is that I did not at all find myself in situations where I see me sweating and running to meet deadlines, me boring myself to tears just to get things done right. I had the space to freestyle and I had targets to work towards. I feel like I got myself with me every step of the way. I even caught myself in those chains of realizing ‘A’ to realizing ‘B’ and on and on, like I was in some sort of master plan. To sum, I’m sure I’ve learnt a lot.

From the bottom of my heart, I very much appreciate being given the chance to be here. Dr. Myers’ plan and feedbacks mean a world to me. No matter what, in me there’s a strong hope to see Dr. Myers later.

Oh no, not long enough. Just 479 words right here. This Reflection Essay should be around 600 to 800 words... Whatever, this is it for now.
